Music

I've been lucky enough to continue playing and performing piano with some of my closest friends in college. I've performed around the states, most recently at Carnegie Hall, Alice Tully Hall, New World Symphony Space and around Columbia's campus. I've always wanted to move to NYC to be around a stronger music community, and I have to say, NYC has not failed me. I always wish I had more time to play, but it's been incredible to live so close to Lincoln Center and Carnegie Hall - I've probably been to more concerts in the last two years than I did in the 18 years I spent in SF.

I recently joined Soh Daiko in NYC, and it's been good to get back on the taiko (Japanese drums) again! I played with San Jose Junior Taiko for 8 years before moving to NYC, performing around the Bay Area. It's something I've done on the side for so many years as a way for me to get some exercise and have fun with friends, but joining Soh Daiko has reminded me of how much I love making music with others.

Travel

As much as I love NYC, I love going on adventures, and have spent the last two years trying to find as many opportunities as possible to get out of NYC (or at the very least, Manhattan). In the last twelve months, I flew to Tokyo, Taipei, Seoul, Paris, Cancun, Miami, Chicago, Boston and Washington DC. I also got to go skiing in Lake Tahoe, camping at Big Sur, and hiking around the Bay Area :)
