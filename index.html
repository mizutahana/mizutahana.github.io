✈ vacationer

Sooo... how much do you need to travel to call yourself a traveler... Definitely need to give thanks to az for letting me to intern abroad for two summers and the two month long winter breaks that Columbia gave us for these incredible experiences.

Growing up in the Bay Area, I often went camping/hiking, and still love to spend time in the great outdoors. Some recent highlights include waking up at 4 am to go chase waterfalls across Bali, canoe-in camping at Utica Reservoir, camping at Big Sur with the tent set up on the edge of a cliff that dropped straight into the Pacific Ocean.

My classical music upbringing has driven me to Western Europe to go listen to the Vienna Opera and the Berlin Philharmonic (Zimerman? Had a solo recital while I was there?!!), and to explore d'Orsay, Uffizi, and the Prado Museum. I guess you can listen to recordings and see all of the artwork from the comforts of your bed at home, but I really don't think any technology comes even close to experiencing these live, in person.

And the food! And the drinks! I think I cried when I tasted the rolled noodles with crispy pork in Bangkok, the most glorious taco in Mexico City, the spicy fish head curry in Singapore that filled my heart with love but also destroyed my stomach (11/10 would eat again), my custom drink(s) in Prague at a menu-less speakeasy, the breakfast in Taipei at a stand that had the audacity to be closed the next day when I tried to go back, and the iskender kebab in Istanbul. I could eat this food everyday!
